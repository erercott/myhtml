Mad at gas car: E2A

The year wasn't 2008 when I received the console and video game that came pre-packaged with the refurbished XBOX 360. My mother mentioned that it was on clearance and made sure to clarify it was the only reason she purchased the console. It was blindgly obvious the console was old. But the menu its UI and the avatars made just turning on the console a pure delight. I got the game Madagascar II: Escape to Africa. It was a film I was familiar with. At the time the only thing I wanted to get my hands on was the newest Batman game...Arkham Asylum. I was so enfatuated, the Madagascar game came as an insult to my inegrity as a gamer.
Madagascar: Escape 2 Africa was a mindless game filled with mini-games. And just like the movies the penguins were the only interesting thing about it. Has anyone seen the show ? Priceless. But, well, this is about the context not the text. I got my XBOX 360 titanium white and with a crappy game for my birthday. Sometime after Barack Obama was sworn into office. I remember where I was when he was elected. (Yes, I only use presidents as a reference point, your move.)


Arm of To

Next up, I had enough cash-money to buy a game shortly after spending an unfortunate amount of hours playing Madagascar 2. I must've felt too attached from home to play any of my PlayStation games. As I felt so out of water being in a new enviornment. I had just moved to the United States and my birthday present was an XBOX360. I had some money and I really wanted to buy that Arkham Asylum game. I had seen DSP play through the whole game. But I had enough for Army of Two. I even remember the GameStop. I had looked through the prices, and Arkham Asylum was twenty bucks over my budget. If it's assumed that I received the game and console near the realease of Arkham Asylum then it would be December of 2009. Since the game came out in August it would still have been priced at $60 dlls, which was typical for new releases at the time.

GameStop - store # 3117 635 Saturn Blvd. San Diego, CA 92154 (closed October 2020)

Note: Well, Army of Two wasn't an incredible let down. I think the Madagascar game came with free XBOX-Live. Army of Two became the video game I had first played online. At least for the time being. I was still too young!


smack down v raw

If memory serves right, a birthday gift which was originally intended to be for christmas(?) was SmackDown vs Raw 2010. What was particular about this Title was the nature of its involvement in my catalogue. I hadn't yet owned a wrestling game for the XBOX 360 and I had completely forgotten about keeping up with the series. Despite having an intimate relationship with the console, I still felt too burnt out by SpiderMan 3's release and I didn't have much say in those days about what to get. In fact, it was moreso maximizing any enjoyment I could muster about a certain topic and staying "in my lane" though there wasn't an enforcement of any type in terms of what I liked. I simply liked what I could and did so without retaliation. My family wouldn't say no. They'd just ignore you. As if you were talking to yourself. I considered their untrustworthy behavior to be taking with extreme precaution, yet the outlier throughout all of this was, well, my grandmother. She was the one to get a hold of SmackDown vs Raw 2010. Despite not knowing that it was a title I had only kept up with during my years as a PlayStation fan, she bridged that gap. None of my other family members would dare care, but I accepted the gift with a not so subtle immature response. I may have even tried to negotiate with my grandmother explaining how I was a bit too old to like wrestling. Nonetheless, I played the hell out of the title and enjoyed drawing comparisons to previous titles. I had grown out of replicating the events and cringy emo create-a-characters that all looked like Dante from Devil May Cry. So I played it. Got annoyed when I tried the online portion of the game. Some dude spammed his create-a-character with repetative kicks. I invited him to an XBOX LIVE party and I distinctly remember his tone of voice. He didn't judge me. He just kicked my ass. A friend of his joined the lobby and they commented on the unique characteristic of being invited to a "party" without a mic. I got enfuriaritngly angry with his tactics-I almost turned red like in a cartoon. And I couldn't speak back. So I just left. Leaving him and his friend to his devices. I may have even rage quit. I wanted a fair bout but I got a crappy game for my birthday.
