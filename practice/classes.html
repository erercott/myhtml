NvMe SSD

Speed:
PCIe Gen 3, 3,500 MB/sec PCIe Gen 4, 7,500 MB/sec
Form Factors: M.2, U.2
Price:
$50 - $200 250 GB - 1 TB
Advantages:
Superior speed, bandwidth, security, end-to-end latency
Disadvantages:
Expensive
Most Common in:
Pre-built desktop PCs or higher-end laptops. (sometimes mid-range)
Used When: Higher transfer speed and bandwidth are top requirements

SATA

Speed:
Up to 550 MB/sec
Form Factors:
2.5-inch drive, M.2, AIC, U.2
Price:
$40 - $140 250 GB - 1TB
Advantages:
Perfect balance between affordability and storage
Disadvantages:
Comparatively slower than NVMe
Most Common in:
Pre-built desktops and budget laptops
Used when:
Higher storage and lower costs.
